about me

Welcome! I'm Lydia Sidhom, an undergraduate at the University of California, Berkeley pursuing degrees in data science and political science.

I enjoy telling stories through data. Bay Area raised, I have worked as a data reporting intern at the San Francisco Chronicle, a Dow Jones News Fund reporting intern at Bay City News, and a data reporter and editor at UC Berkeley’s independent, student-run newspaper The Daily Californian.

I’m interested in the role of data in the U.S. criminal legal system. I worked on a project at UC Berkeley’s Goldman School of Public Policy’s Possibility Lab that helped expedite criminal case resentencing in California through building a data automation tool that sped up the case review process. The project was awarded the Microsoft Catalyst Grant, and the tool is now being used in 12 District Attorney offices across the state. I’m currently working as a data scientist for the U.S. Department of Justice Bureau of Prisons to investigate the possibility of using re-conviction instead of re-arrest as a proxy for a defendant recommitting a crime in a federal recidivism algorithm.

I'm also passionate about data science and computer science pedagogy. At Berkeley, I was on the teaching staff of Data 8, Berkeley's foundational Python and statistics course, for multiple semesters. I also worked as a curriculum developer for the Human Contexts and Ethics wing of Berkeley's College of Computing, Data Science, and Society, as well as for the undergraduate legal studies course Data, Prediction, and Law.
